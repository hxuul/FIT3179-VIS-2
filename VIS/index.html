ABSTUDY 1: Provides financial assistance to Indigenous Australian students and apprentices to help with living costs while studying or training.
ABSTUDY 2: Offers support for education-related expenses other than living costs to Indigenous Australian students, such as school fees and educational materials.
Age Pension: Income support for older Australians who have reached the qualifying age to assist with basic living expenses during retirement.
Austudy: Financial assistance for full-time students and apprentices aged 25 or over to help with living costs while they study or train.
Carer Allowance 1: A supplementary payment for people who provide additional daily care and attention to someone with a disability or medical condition.
Carer Allowance 2: Provides a Health Care Card for carers of children under 16 with a disability or medical condition, giving access to concessions and reduced-cost medicines.
Carer Payment: Income support for individuals who are unable to work full-time because they care for someone with a severe disability, illness, or frailty due to age.
Rent Assistance: A non-taxable income supplement to help eligible people who rent in the private market or community housing with their rental costs.
Seniors Health Card: Provides access to cheaper health care and some discounts for self-funded retirees of pension age who do not qualify for the Age Pension.
Disability Pension: Financial support for people with a permanent physical, intellectual, or psychiatric condition that prevents them from working.
Family Benefit A: Assistance to families with the cost of raising children, paid per child based on age and family income.
Family Benefit B: Additional assistance for single-parent families and families with one main income earner to help with the cost of raising children.
Health Care Card: Provides access to reduced-cost prescription medicines and other concessions to eligible low-income earners and recipients of certain government payments.
JobSeeker Payment: Financial help for people aged 22 and over who are unemployed or temporarily unable to work due to illness or injury.
Low Income Card: A concession card that offers reduced-cost medicines and other concessions to individuals and families on a low income.
Parenting Pay 1: Income support for partnered parents or guardians caring for young children, assisting with living expenses.
Parenting Pay 2: Income support for single parents or guardians with young children to help with living costs.
Pensioner Concession: Provides access to cheaper health services and medicines for eligible pensioners.
Special Benefit: Financial assistance for people in severe financial hardship who are not eligible for other income support payments.
Youth Allowance 1: Financial help for young people aged 16 to 21 who are looking for work, undertaking approved activities, or temporarily unable to work.
Youth Allowance 2: Financial assistance for full-time students and apprentices aged 16 to 24 to help with living costs while studying or training.
